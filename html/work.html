HOW DOES AI WORK

Artificial Intelligence (AI) works by mimicking how humans think, learn, and make decisions. At its core, AI systems use large amounts of data, identify patterns, and make predictions or take actions based on that data. The process often begins with data collection, which could include anything from images and text to numbers and sensor inputs. This data is then processed using algorithms—step-by-step instructions or mathematical models—that help the machine learn from it. In supervised learning, for example, the system is trained on labeled data (where the correct answer is already known), allowing it to learn the relationship between input and output. In unsupervised learning, the AI tries to find hidden patterns without specific guidance. Reinforcement learning allows AI to learn through trial and error, receiving rewards or penalties based on its actions. Over time, the AI improves its performance by adjusting its internal parameters to reduce errors. The more data and experience it gets, the better it becomes at making accurate predictions or decisions, which is how modern AI powers technologies like voice assistants, recommendation systems, self-driving cars, and more.
