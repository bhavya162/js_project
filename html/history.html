HISTORY OF ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE

history of artificial intelligence (AI), a survey of important events and people in the field of artificial intelligence (AI) from the early work of British logician Alan Turing in the 1930s to advancements at the turn of the 21st century. AI is the ability of a digital computer or computer-controlled robot to perform tasks commonly associated with intelligent beings. The term is frequently applied to the project of developing systems endowed with the intellectual processes characteristic of humans, such as the ability to reason, discover meaning, generalize, or learn from past experience. For modern developments in AI, see artificial intelligence.

1940s–1950s: The Birth of AI

1943 – First concept of a neural network by McCulloch and Pitts. 1950 – Alan Turing proposes the Turing Test to measure machine intelligence. 1956 – The term “Artificial Intelligence” is officially coined by John McCarthy at the Dartmouth Conference, considered the birth of AI as a field.

1950s–1970s: Early Growth & Optimism

Development of early AI programs that could solve puzzles and play games like chess.ELIZA (1966) – One of the first chatbot programs. Researchers believed human-level AI was near, leading to high expectations.

1970s–1980s: The First AI Winter

Progress slowed due to limited computing power, and AI couldn’t meet the hype. Funding and interest dropped—this period is known as the AI Winter.

1980s: Expert Systems & Revival

AI regained popularity with Expert Systems (e.g., MYCIN), which used rule-based logic to mimic human experts. Still, limitations in handling uncertainty led to a second AI winter in the late 1980s.

1990s–2000s: Machine Learning Era

AI shifted focus to statistical methods and data-driven learning. 1997 – IBM’s Deep Blue defeated world chess champion Garry Kasparov. Rise of support vector machines and Bayesian networks.

2010s–Present: Deep Learning & Modern AI

Massive data and GPU-powered computing enabled deep learning (neural networks with many layers). 2012 – Breakthrough in image recognition using deep learning by AlexNet. 2016 – Google’s AlphaGo defeated Go champion Lee Sedol, a major milestone.

AI became part of daily life: Siri, Alexa, self-driving cars, recommendation systems.

2020s–Now: Generative AI and Future Directions

Rise of Generative AI (e.g., ChatGPT, DALL·E, Sora). AI is used in medicine, art, education, business, and entertainment. Ongoing debates around ethics, job automation, AI safety, and regulation.
